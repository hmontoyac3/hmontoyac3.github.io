What's missing from the list

The list has no one from the most useful profile for RP specifically: empirical researchers who have recently published survey-based or observational work in Research Policy. That is who a RP Associate Editor would actually assign this to — innovation economists who use survey data at the firm level (CIS-based, OECD STAN), scholars who have published in RP on management practices and productivity using multi-firm observational designs. These are the people whose feedback would most accurately simulate what peer review will look like. They are absent from the list entirely.
