Heterogeneous Effects of Theory-Based Entrepreneurship Training

For whom does scientific-approach training work — and why?
↗ GitHub repo ↗ Analysis scripts ← Back to website

What this paper is about

We use a pooled dataset of six randomized controlled trials (Italy, Colombia, Netherlands, Spain, UK) from the IdeaBoosterLab / ERC program to ask: which founders benefit most from theory-based entrepreneurship training?

The program randomly assigned founders to one of three arms — Pure Control, Theory-and-Evidence (T&E), and Evidence-based — all of which attended sessions, but with different content. The key comparison is content type, not program vs. no program.

The outcome of interest is Scientific Intensity (SI): a composite score (1–5) measuring how scientifically the founder approaches business decisions — testable hypotheses, structured experimentation, evidence-based pivots.

Analysis pipeline

Script 01 Pooled RF on SI level

Y = si_overall (level, all periods pooled)

Z = all available variables including dosage (attendance, num_interv)

Purpose: first pass — which variables have any predictive signal at all?

Limitation: pools all periods and arms together; mixes level with change; dosage is post-assignment; not suitable for HTE discovery.

Script 02 RF on ΔSI by arm and period

Y = ΔSI = si_overallt − si_overall0 (change from baseline)

Z = baseline characteristics only — no dosage (post-assignment, not pre-treatment)

Structure: 14 separate models — 3 arms × 5 periods (Pure Control p5 skipped, N=44)

Purpose: find where variable importance diverges across arms → those are candidate HTE moderators for DML.

Script 01 result — what predicts SI in general

Top predictors of SI level: founding_team_size, int_startup_phase, hours_worked, employees, prob_unexpectedevents_2Y/6M, illusion_of_control.

RMSE = 0.86 on a 1–5 scale (CV-MSE = 0.74, held-out 30% test set).

What this does not mean: high importance ≠ the treatment works better for those founders. founding_team_size being #1 means larger teams score higher on SI in general — not that the training is more effective for them. This is a prediction exercise, not a causal estimate.

Script 02 result — how importance evolves over time

Averaged across arms, prob_unexpectedevents_6M, idea_breadth_h, and idea_aspiration_1/2 are consistently the strongest predictors of improvement. But averaging across arms hides the most interesting signal — see the divergence figure below.

The key finding — divergence by arm

For each of 6 candidate variables, this figure shows how strongly that variable predicts who improves their SI within each arm, across periods. When the lines diverge, the variable predicts differently by arm — that is the signal for treatment effect heterogeneity.

Reading the figure

For each panel: if the three lines are close together, the variable predicts improvement equally across all arms — not a moderator of interest. Where lines separate, the variable predicts improvement differently depending on what content the founder received.

What stands out

illusion_of_control — The red line (T&E) is consistently higher than the other two across all 5 periods. Founders who overestimate their control over outcomes at baseline improve their SI more specifically in the T&E arm. This is consistent with theory: T&E content was designed to calibrate exactly that bias. Strongest DML candidate.
new_ideas — The red line spikes dramatically in period 3 (importance ≈ 94 in T&E vs. ~47–52 in other arms), then converges. Founders open to new ideas at baseline show the largest SI gains in T&E specifically around the mid-program period. The effect appears transitional — DML should be tested at period 3 specifically.
future_ev_influence — Orange (Pure Control) starts at 100 in period 1 and declines; red (T&E) rises to ~81 by period 5. Founders who believe external factors shape their business improve naturally in Control early on, but by the end of the program that belief is specifically predictive in T&E — possibly because T&E teaches how to act on it scientifically.
prob_unexpectedevents_6M — All three lines track closely together. High everywhere, does not diverge. Strong predictor of ΔSI regardless of arm → use as a control variable in DML, not as a primary moderator.
Important caveat: these are predictive patterns, not causal estimates. A variable predicting ΔSI more strongly in T&E than in Control could still reflect selection or differential attrition — not a treatment-driven effect. The next step (DML) resolves this by using the RCT structure to estimate causal conditional average treatment effects.
