What this analysis does

The Random Forest analysis (RF log) told us which baseline variables predict who improves their scientific intensity (ΔSI) — and found that illusion_of_control and future_ev_influence ranked consistently high in the Theory-and-Evidence arm but not in Pure Control.

That is a predictive signal, not a causal one. A variable ranking higher in T&E than in Control could reflect selection or differential attrition — not a genuine moderator of the treatment effect.

This DML step asks the causal question: after controlling for all baseline characteristics, does a founder's score on variable W causally change how much they benefit from T&E?

Why does the ATE fade? Likely a combination of attrition (N drops from ~450 to ~136 by period 5) and mean-reversion in scientific intensity as the program recedes. The treatment content was delivered in the early periods; the SI measure captures an immediate learning response that dissipates over time. Investigating the fade-out mechanism is a next step.

Statistical logic: if ATE ≈ 0 in periods 3–5, there is no heterogeneity to detect. Running DML there would only find noise. The primary analysis focuses on periods 1–2.

GATES — Group Average Treatment Effects

GATES splits the sample into quintiles by predicted CATE (= ATE + β₁ × W_c) and estimates the treatment effect within each quintile. A gradient from Q1 to Q5 confirms that heterogeneity in β₁ is real and not driven by a single outlier.

Interpretation and next steps

What DML found that RF did not

The RF showed illusion_of_control diverging across arms — it predicted ΔSI more strongly in T&E. DML shows this is predictive, not causal: IoC does not significantly amplify the treatment effect once we control for all other baseline characteristics.

By contrast, future_ev_influence — a belief that external factors can shape the venture — does show significant causal moderation (β₁=+0.48, p=0.018) in period 1. This makes theoretical sense: T&E teaches founders to use scientific thinking to navigate an uncertain environment. Founders who already believe the environment matters are likely more primed to apply that framework.

Limitations

  • W is demeaned, not residualized. A fully rigorous BLP would partial out X from W as well. The current approach (demeaned W) is standard for exploratory analysis but understates the precision of β₁. Flag for confirmatory run.
  • Missing data imputation. Nine variables were 100% missing in the T&E + Control subsample and dropped; remaining missings mean-imputed. Check which variables and whether imputation affects results.
  • Sample size. N=438 in period 1 for future_ev_influence; ~88 per quintile. CLAN comparisons are exploratory.
  • Multiple comparisons. 8 BLP tests run. Bonferroni-corrected threshold would be p<0.006 — the p=0.018 finding does not survive. It is a candidate for pre-registration, not a confirmed result.

Next steps

  • Pre-register future_ev_influence as the primary moderator hypothesis.
  • Re-run BLP with residualized W (partial out X from W using Lasso) for the confirmatory analysis.
  • Investigate the ATE fade-out: why does T&E's effect disappear by period 3?
  • Expand scanner to remaining RF candidate variables and the full period 1–5 range as robustness.
