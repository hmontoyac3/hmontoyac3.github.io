Method: GenericML (Chernozhukov et al., Econometrica 2025) run separately by category — variable importance to select moderators → interaction OLS. Reference: McKenzie et al., VoxDevLit 2025.

Where we stand — May 2026

What we have. (1) Mediation confirmed: T→ΔSI→pivot is a real causal chain. ΔSI mediates 21% of the pivot ATE at P1 (Sobel p = 0.006) and 26% at P2 (p = 0.009). Direct effect of T on pivot survives (β ≈ 0.12). Scientific thinking is a genuine mechanism. (2) Control arm deterioration: 59–65% of control founders have negative ΔSI at P1–P2. Training partly prevents a natural deterioration of scientific thinking, not just adds to it. Founders who believe more in unknown external factors (“future_ev_influence”) decline most without training. (3) Attrition: training retains founders dramatically (T=37% dropout before P2 vs C=68%). Retention benefit is uniform; only exception: high-exit-probability founders benefit less. (4) Individual HTE is null across all 12 moderators, all methods (480+ cells, 3 estimators). Uniform learning is robust — all founder types improve equally.

Literature context: pivots. Camuffo & Gambardella have already published pivots as an outcome in three papers: Management Science 2020 (treated founders pivot 2× more; secondary outcome); SMJ 2024 large-scale replication (nonlinear effect — trained firms pivot 1–2 times focusedly, neither zero nor excessively); Strategy Science 2024 (Valentine, Novelli, Agarwal — pivot types via qualitative coding of 1,600+ interviews: theorization × experimentation produces purposeful pivots); SMJ 2024 (Novelli & Spina — HTE on performance by business model maturity, with pivot as mechanism, not outcome). What has not been published: HTE on the pivot effect using individual-level psychological moderators. The mediation chain T→ΔSI→pivot is also not formally established in any of these papers.

New angles (May 2026). Five non-HTE heterogeneity dimensions tested (script 17): (1) Variance of ΔSI: training does not equalise or polarise — SD identical T vs C (Bartlett p=0.29). The uniform mean is a uniform distribution. (2) Trajectory maintenance P1→P2: gains fade equally for everyone (ATE on maintenance = 0.016, p=0.87). No founder type sustains learning better. (3) SI sub-index composition: all five sub-indices move significantly, but Evidence collection (d=0.53) and Evaluation (d=0.48) move most; Theory/hypothesis generation least (d=0.33). The empirical side of SDM is more teachable than the theoretical side. (4) Pivot count and quality: training adds +1.04 pivots on average (p<0.001); treated founders are 34pp more likely to pivot ≥2 times (57.7% vs 24.1%). Category distribution shifts toward more substantive pivots (categories 2–3). Replicates SMJ 2024 nonlinear pattern with individual-level data. (5) ΔSI × attrition: learning does not predict retention. Marginal signal: within treated arm, higher ΔSI slightly increases dropout by P3 (T×ΔSI p=0.085) — learning more may liberate founders to exit with conviction, not retain them.

Way forward. Still waiting for Diego’s cognitive battery (risk aversion, uncertainty aversion, learning orientation, competitiveness — 17 items). With that: either HTE paper if signal emerges, or mechanism + null paper if confirmed null. Strongest current contributions without Diego: (a) T→ΔSI→pivot partial mediation (21–26%), (b) prevention-of-deterioration story (60% control negative ΔSI), (c) SI sub-index profile (evidence > theory in teachability), (d) pivot count/quality replication with individual-level data.

2026-05-16
Four new routes completed. Route 1 (Mediation T→ΔSI→pivot): strong partial mediation confirmed. ΔSI mediates 21% of the pivot effect at P1 (ACME = 0.033, Sobel p = 0.006) and 26% at P2 (ACME = 0.039, Sobel p = 0.009); direct effect of T on pivot remains significant (c′ ≈ 0.12). No mediation on log_sales (total effect on sales is null). Scientific thinking improvement is a genuine mechanism for behavioral change. Route 2 (Translation HTE): within treated arm, ΔSI → pivot is significant at P1 for 92% of moderator runs. Planning orientation moderates translation: high-planning founders translate learning into pivots more at P1 (β = +0.049, p = 0.015) but reverse by P2 (β = −0.086, p < 0.001); IoC borderline at P2 (p = 0.066). Route 4 (Control dynamics): 59–65% of control arm has negative ΔSI at P1–P2. Predictors of deeper decline: future_ev_influence (β = −0.37, p = 0.041) and months_working_1 (β = −0.009, p = 0.086) — founders who believe in unknown external factors deteriorate more without training. Route 5 (Attrition HTE): training reduces dropout by 31pp before P2 (T=37% vs C=68%). Attrition benefit is uniform across founder types; one exception: high exit-probability founders benefit less from retention (Int β = +0.113, p = 0.012 for dropout-by-P3).
