Framework — Three Categories

Person-level
SDM capability, ambiguity aversion, learning orientation, experience, illusion of control, uncertainty beliefs
Venture-level
BM development stage, sector, team composition, idea scope
Dosage
Attendance, sessions completed, mentor engagement

Method: GenericML (Chernozhukov et al., Econometrica 2025) run separately by category — variable importance to select moderators → interaction OLS. Reference: McKenzie et al., VoxDevLit 2025.

Working on

HTE verdict (May 2026): Individual-level heterogeneity is null across all methods — D5 (480 cells, lasso BLP), D6 (5-learner ensemble), D7 (official GenericML, 6 learners incl. XGBoost). Two D7 borderline hits on log(sales) at P2 (fei_resid, months_working_1) do not survive lasso-only spec (p > 0.5 both): artifacts of better B(Z) estimation, not real HTE. Site-level HTE (Ruta 5): ATEs differ significantly across sites 9–13 (Wald p = 0.0006 for ΔSI P1), but this replicates Jannace et al.’s appendix and is explained by known implementation quality differences (attendance proxy r ≈ 1 with site ATE). Not a new contribution.

Five new routes under analysis: (1) Mediation: Does T→ΔSI→pivot/sales hold? Test whether scientific thinking improvement is the mechanism linking training to behavioral outcomes (Baron–Kenny + causal mediation). (2) Translation HTE: Among treated founders, who converts learning into action? OLS within treated arm: pivot ~ ΔSI × moderator — identifies whether some types translate better. (3) Null as theory test: SDM is designed to move everyone uniformly; the homogeneous ATE is a structural prediction of the intervention, not a failure to find HTE. (4) Control arm dynamics: Why does ΔSI go negative in control at P1–P3? Within-control OLS: which founder characteristics predict SI decline? (5) Attrition as outcome: Does training differentially retain certain founder types? HTE on program dropout across the same 12 moderators. Still waiting for Diego’s cognitive battery (risk aversion, uncertainty aversion, learning orientation, competitiveness).

Progress Log

2026-05-16
Four new routes completed. Route 1 (Mediation T→ΔSI→pivot): strong partial mediation confirmed. ΔSI mediates 21% of the pivot effect at P1 (ACME = 0.033, Sobel p = 0.006) and 26% at P2 (ACME = 0.039, Sobel p = 0.009); direct effect of T on pivot remains significant (c′ ≈ 0.12). No mediation on log_sales (total effect on sales is null). Scientific thinking improvement is a genuine mechanism for behavioral change. Route 2 (Translation HTE): within treated arm, ΔSI → pivot is significant at P1 for 92% of moderator runs. Planning orientation moderates translation: high-planning founders translate learning into pivots more at P1 (β = +0.049, p = 0.015) but reverse by P2 (β = −0.086, p < 0.001); IoC borderline at P2 (p = 0.066). Route 4 (Control dynamics): 59–65% of control arm has negative ΔSI at P1–P2. Predictors of deeper decline: future_ev_influence (β = −0.37, p = 0.041) and months_working_1 (β = −0.009, p = 0.086) — founders who believe in unknown external factors deteriorate more without training. Route 5 (Attrition HTE): training reduces dropout by 31pp before P2 (T=37% vs C=68%). Attrition benefit is uniform across founder types; one exception: high exit-probability founders benefit less from retention (Int β = +0.113, p = 0.012 for dropout-by-P3).

HTE verdict closed.
