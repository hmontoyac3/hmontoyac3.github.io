Working Papers
Measuring Scientific Decision-Making in Organizations
with Arnaldo Camuffo and Thomaz Teodorovicz
Presents and validates a 24-item survey instrument measuring scientific decision-making (SDM) across three components: causal evidence (theorizing), empirical evidence (gathering and analyzing data), and adaptive learning (updating based on data). Deployed to 3,000+ individuals across 57 firms. SDM is found to be a highly individual-level trait (ICC = 1.8%). Higher-tier managers score significantly higher on adaptive learning relative to frontline managers (b = 0.14–0.16, p < 0.01), with no significant differences on the other two components.
N = 3,000+ 57 firms
Heterogeneous Effects of Theory-Based Entrepreneurship Training
with Arnaldo Camuffo and Alfonso Gambardella
Examines heterogeneous treatment effects of theory-based training programs across five RCTs (Italy, Colombia, Netherlands, Spain, UK) using Random Forest and causal inference methods.
5 RCTs In progress
Extracting Interpretable Models from Large Language Models for Organizational Data
with Said Kazemi
Compares classical econometric approaches (LASSO) with LLM-based methods for predicting ordinal organizational variables. Explores linear probing, prompt-ensemble, and linear distillation techniques.
